Andres Barrioquinto

Andres Barrioquinto

Surrealistic portraits, layered with Japanese patterns.

Andres Barrioquinto (b.1975) is known for surrealistic, layered portraits that incorporate Japanese ukiyo-e patterns and hyperrealist portraiture. Notable auction record: Procession (2019) sold for approx. $359,777. Exhibitions include Tang Contemporary Art (Bangkok) and a 2018 National Museum "Portraits" exhibition; a monograph on his work was published in 2022.

Elmer Borlongan

Elmer Misa Borlongan

Figurative expressionism focused on everyday life and resilience.

Elmer Borlongan (b.1967) paints stylized figures showing daily life and resilience. Awards include the CCP 13 Artists Award (1994) and major retrospectives at the Metropolitan Museum and the Ayala Museum. Auction highlights include Quiapo (2016) sold by Leon Gallery for six-figure sums.

Source
Leslie de Chavez

Leslie de Chavez

Expressive paintings and installations with social commentary.

Leslie de Chavez (b.1978) explores social and political themes in expressive paintings and installations. Represented by Arario Gallery since 2006, De Chavez has shown widely across Asia and curated community projects such as Project Space Pilipinas.

Ronald Ventura

Ronald Ventura

A leader among younger Filipino contemporary artists; mixed media, sculpture and painting.

Ronald Ventura (b.1973) combines hyperrealism, pop culture and graffiti. Auction records include Party Animal (2021) at Christie's HK and multiple high-value sales; recent retrospectives and major exhibitions have been held across Asia and internationally.

Source
Rodel Tapaya

Rodel Tapaya-Garcia

Narrative painting blending folk tales and contemporary life.

Rodel Tapaya (b.1980) blends Philippine folklore and contemporary narratives into colorful, narrative paintings. He won the Signature Art Prize (2011) and has exhibited at Yavuz Gallery, Jack Bell (London) and Tang Contemporary Art; his work often references myth, legend and moral storytelling.
