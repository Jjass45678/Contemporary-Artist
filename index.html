Andres Barrioquinto

Andres Barrioquinto

Surrealistic portraits, layered with Japanese patterns.

Andres Barrioquinto (b.1975) creates surreal, layered portraits that combine Western technique with Japanese ukiyo-e influences and Filipino iconography. His use of familiar faces, devotional poses and patterned backgrounds connects to Philippine visual traditions and contemporary Filipino identity. Notable auction record: Procession (2019) sold for approx. $359,777. Exhibitions include Tang Contemporary Art (Bangkok) and the National Museum (Portraits, 2018).

Figurative expressionism focused on everyday life and resilience.

Elmer Borlongan (b.1967) portrays everyday Filipinos with empathy â€” his figures, urban scenes and market settings reflect social histories, community resilience and local rituals. Borlongan's work is rooted in Filipino lived experience and civic memory. Awards include the CCP 13 Artists Award (1994) and retrospectives at the Metropolitan Museum and Ayala Museum.

Leslie de Chavez

Leslie de Chavez

Expressive paintings and installations with social commentary.

Leslie de Chavez (b.1978) creates bold, socially engaged works that reference Philippine history, faith and power structures. Through expressive canvases and installations he interrogates how colonial legacies and religious iconography shape Filipino social values and collective memory.

Rodel Tapaya

Rodel Tapaya-Garcia

Narrative painting blending folk tales and contemporary life.

Rodel Tapaya (b.1980) transforms folk tales, myths and ritual imagery into contemporary narratives that speak to Filipino cultural memory. His compositions revisit origin stories, community myths and moral parables, making traditional motifs relevant to modern audiences.
